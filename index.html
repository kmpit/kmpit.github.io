Personal Website

Personal Project | Apr - May 2020

Designing and developing a fully responsive site to hone the skills I'm passionate about.

Skills: Design for mobile & desktop, copywriting, responsive frontend web development
Tools: Dev: HTML, CSS, JS; Design: Pen and paper, Figma

In creating this site, I've leveled up my design and development skills in so many ways. Here's a bit of my process:

  • Started with a desire to completely redesign my personal website and a bunch of ideas of what I wanted:
  • Stepped back to research priorities of users (employers and peers) via career development resources and a survey of best-in-class personal websites; this research defined key elements such as the order of contents
  • Sketched numerous design alternatives for each section, leveraging techniques such as Crazy 8s to spark creativity

A few examples of early sketches for the Projects section are below (though there are more where these came from!)

  • Selected best aspects of various design alternatives and used Figma to flesh out realistic options for selection

For the Experience section, I prototyped three key design options.

I selected Option C because it best allows for both a high-level overview and detailed job descriptions, both of which research showed to be important. Peers also responded positively to the time-based structure and interactivity.

  • Coded site in HTML, CSS, and JS, problem-solving to implement features such as snap-scrolling mobile carousel (Experience), responsive cards (Project)
  • Iterated on designs to improve appearance and usability based on continuously sourced feedback
  • Tested thoroughly to ensure strong functionality and appearance in all device sizes, browsers
  • Assessed usability of final site with user tests and iterated based on results; for example, mitigated user confusion by adding scrollbar to modal

I learned a ton about design and development from this project. For example:

  • When designing for an informational site, it's important to fully account for the content to be delivered; unlike web apps, where content appears in consistent formats, informational content may be any length, and the design must properly frame and support content of all lengths
  • The size of content relative to the screen is another hugely important parameter to consider early in design
  • An organized approach to styling and repsonsive breakpoints saves you time down the road
  • 'Cool' things, like buttons that jump up on hover, are easy to code; the more difficult, but more important, aspects of a site are the ones you don't notice, like proper margins and spacing across all browser sizes

I can't wait to keep improving on this site and to continue building my skills so that I can build even more functional and beautiful sites in the future.

What-to-Plan

UW | Fall 2017

Leveraging iterative design, user research, and 3D product design to tackle pain points related to time management.

Skills: Problem definition, user research (surveys), physical product design, usability testing
Tools: Iterative design process, QFD, persona, Solidworks, 3D printing
Collaborators: Conrad Heidebrecht, Katrina Hermanns, Sylvia George, Jack Yu, Vlad Hociota

  • Created time management system in which users track to-dos using a mobile app and receive reminders in the form of light/audio notifications from portable, attachable notification beacons
  • Headed physical product design, including creation of MFP through modeling in Solidworks and 3D printing

Our thorough design process led to a well-researched final product, from user research to concept development to iterating on the final prototype.

  • Started by exploring current solutions and frustrations via user surveys; results informed us that both digital and physical systems served certain needs, but users felt current solutions were easy to forget, difficult to use
  • Further understood users by conducting interviews and creating personas, which revealed key needs: easy to use, effortless to remember, smooth to incorporate into existing habits
  • Also assessed competitors and established benchmarks using QFD
  • Based on problem definition work, collaboratively brainstormed solutions, using morphological chart to generate three distinct concepts
  • Using best of class chart and synthesizing two of initial concepts, developed concept of planing app combined with physical notification devices
  • This idea also addressed areas of need as identified by competitive analysis and user research
  • Developed three distinct prototypes for physical beacon
  • Conducted usability testing to uncover issues with ergonomics, affordances; also conducted testing to determine most understandable color coding for beacon's lights
  • Iterated based on feedback and communicated final design clearly through 3D-printed prototype and design drawings

Given the opportunity to continue this project, the next step would be further testing, ideally through a long-term study with field observation and periodic interviews.
